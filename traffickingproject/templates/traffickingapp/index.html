Human Trafficking

Taking action on Utah's human trafficking problem

What is Human Trafficking?

A type of modern-day slavery. Where a victim gets forced, tricked, or groomed into forced labor, domestic servitude, or sex trafficking. Anyone can be a victim of human trafficking, but some people are more vulnerable. Women, runaway and homeless youth, undocumented immigrants, and individuals who identify as LGBTQ are more at risk. Human trafficking is a local and global problem. Lots of people are being moved and trafficked in the United States, some people are being trafficked and circled in their hometowns

What is happening in Utah?

There are many cases in Utah, some recent ones that were caught were commercial sex, multi-state prostitution rings, illicit massage businesses, labor trafficking in the agricultural sectors, and much more. Utah connects with many states making it possible for thousands of victims to be moved through the state.

Utah Statistics

Based on the human trafficking hotline in 2020 they received 182 calls from Utah, with 64 human trafficking cases reported. In 2016 they received 156 calls and 105 cases were reported. 72 of those victims were adults and 26 were minors. 88 of them were female, 21 of them were male, and 1 of them was a gender minority. 37 of them were legal in the United States, while 27 were foreign nationals.

They also found that the top 5 sex trafficking venues were:
  1. Online ads
  2. Residental Brothels
  3. Pornography
  4. Hotel/Motel based
  5. Truck Stop based

The top 3 labor trafficking venues were:
  1. Traveling Sales Crew
  2. Domestic Work
  3. Forest/Reforestation
