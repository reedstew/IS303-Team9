How to help

Ways to help Utah's victims and survivors of human trafficking

Know the Signs

There are multiple signs to watch out for to be able to identify human trafficking, some of those are (higher list visit humantraffickinghotline.org):

    • Someone you know is offered a job opportunity that seems too good to be true
    • Someone you know is recruited for an opportunity that requires them to move far away, but their recruiter or prospective employer avoids answering their questions or is reluctant to provide detailed information about the job
    • A potential employer refuses to give workers a signed contract or asks them to sign a contract in a language they do not understand
    • Employees are living where they work or are transported by guards between home and workplace
    • Employees do not have control of their passport or other identity documents

Who to Contact


Call the Human trafficking hotline at 888–373–7888
    National Hotline serves victims and survivors of human trafficking and the anti-trafficking community across the United States and its territories, 24/7, in more than 200 languages. Contact the confidential hotline to report tips, seek services, ask for help, or receive information and statistics related to human trafficking

For the local Utah Human trafficking tip line call 801–200–3443
    This is a 24-hour tip line run by the Utah Attorney General’s office. Call the tip line to report tips about human trafficking. The tip line is answered by a voicemail machine.

Donate

Help Survivors of human trafficking by donating to Aspen Magdalene House. This house in Salt Lake City will help survivors recover and get back on their feet. Click here to learn more and donate.
